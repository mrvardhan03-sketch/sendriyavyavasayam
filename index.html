Organic Farming

Agriculture is a crucial part of human life, providing food, employment, and raw materials. In recent years, organic farming has gained attention as a sustainable and healthier alternative to conventional farming. Organic farming avoids synthetic chemicals and focuses on natural methods to grow crops and raise animals. This helps protect the environment, improve soil health, and produce safer food.

Organic farming avoids the use of synthetic chemicals and genetically modified seeds. Instead, it relies on natural fertilizers such as compost, vermicompost, green manure, and cow dung, along with crop rotation and biological pest control. These natural fertilizers enrich the soil with essential nutrients, improve its structure, and promote beneficial microbial activity, making the soil healthier and more productive over time.

Key Features

  • No Chemicals: No chemical fertilizers, pesticides, or harmful additives.
  • Natural Inputs: Uses compost, cow dung, green manure, neem oil, and natural pest control.
  • Soil Health Focus: Crop rotation, mulching, composting, and healthy microorganisms.
  • Eco-Friendly: Reduces pollution, protects water bodies, and maintains biodiversity.
